Entry 01  ·  Written Mode

The Seat Next to Me

There used to be someone who always sat next to me. It wasn't something we ever planned or talked about; it just happened from the first week and slowly became routine. The same bench, the same side, every day. We weren't very talkative, but there was a quiet understanding between us. We shared notes, made small comments during lectures, and sometimes laughed at things that weren't even that funny. It was simple, but it felt comfortable.

One day, they didn't come to class. I didn't think much of it at first, because missing a day is normal. I sat in the same place, leaving the seat next to me empty without really questioning it. But the next day, when they returned, something felt different. They didn't sit next to me. Instead, they chose another bench, with different people.

It was such a small change that no one else seemed to notice. Even I tried to ignore it. For a moment, I thought about asking them why, but the question felt unnecessary for something so small. So I said nothing, and they said nothing too.

Days passed, and that small change became permanent. We still saw each other, exchanged occasional greetings, but the comfort we once had disappeared. There was no explanation, no clear reason — just distance that quietly settled in.

Sometimes I still wonder if I did something wrong, or if it was just a random choice that meant nothing to them but everything to me. But I never asked, and now it feels like it's too late.

The seat next to me is still there. Someone else might sit in it now, but it never feels the same. Not because it's empty… but because it's no longer what it used to be.

Reflection

The purpose of this story is to capture how small, everyday changes in behavior can impact relationships without any clear explanation. It focuses on a subtle shift in a familiar situation and how the absence of conversation can create emotional distance.

Entry 02  ·  Written Mode

The Spaces Between Words

Not everything that shapes our relationships is spoken out loud. In fact, some of the most important parts of our interactions exist quietly — in pauses, in tone, and in the small shifts we notice but rarely address.

There are moments when everything appears normal on the surface. Conversations continue, routines stay the same, and nothing seems clearly wrong. Yet, something feels different. A reply is shorter than usual, a laugh feels slightly forced, or a familiar conversation no longer flows as easily. These changes are subtle, almost unnoticeable to others, but to the person experiencing them, they can feel significant.

What makes these moments complicated is their uncertainty. They are not strong enough to question directly, yet they are too present to ignore. This leaves people in a space where they are constantly thinking, trying to understand whether something has actually changed or whether they are simply overanalyzing ordinary situations.

In many cases, people choose not to address these feelings. It can feel unnecessary to bring up something that cannot be clearly explained. There is also a fear of being wrong — of creating a problem where none exists. As a result, silence becomes the easier option. Interactions continue, but with an unspoken layer of doubt beneath them.

Over time, these small uncertainties can slowly create distance. Not because of any major conflict, but because of a lack of clarity. Without open understanding, assumptions begin to take the place of reality. People start interpreting actions based on feelings rather than facts, and this can quietly change the way they connect with each other.

This reflects an important aspect of communication that is often overlooked — it is not limited to words alone. Communication also exists in how people respond, how present they are, and how consistently they engage with others. When these elements change, even slightly, the impact can be felt, even if it is never discussed.

In the end, it is often the smallest, most unnoticed moments that shape our understanding of others. The things left unsaid, the questions left unasked, and the feelings left unexpressed can influence relationships more than we realize. Recognizing this does not always mean confronting every doubt, but it does highlight the importance of awareness, clarity, and the willingness to understand beyond what is immediately visible.
